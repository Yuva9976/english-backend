# English Grammar

Grammar is the way we arrange words to make proper sentences. Understanding grammar helps you speak and write English correctly and clearly.

---

## What is Grammar?

Grammar describes the rules that help us put words together to make sentences. It shows us how to speak and write clearly so others can understand us.

**Why Grammar Matters:**
- Helps us share ideas clearly
- Allows us to ask questions properly
- Helps us understand other people
- Makes our writing professional
- Improves communication skills

---

## 1. Parts of Speech

These are the basic building blocks of English sentences.

### üìò Verbs and Verb Tenses
**Action verbs** express what is happening (run, work, study, write).  
**State verbs** express a situation or feeling (be, have, know, like).

**Common Verb Tenses:**
- **Present Simple:** I work every day
- **Present Continuous:** I am working now
- **Past Simple:** I worked yesterday
- **Past Continuous:** I was working at 5pm
- **Present Perfect:** I have worked here for 5 years
- **Future Simple:** I will work tomorrow

### üìó Nouns
Nouns represent **people** (teacher, John), **places** (city, London), and **things** (table, music).

**Types of Nouns:**
- **Common Nouns:** book, car, dog
- **Proper Nouns:** London, Mary, Monday
- **Countable Nouns:** one apple, two apples
- **Uncountable Nouns:** water, rice, information
- **Collective Nouns:** team, family, group

### üìô Adjectives
Adjectives tell us more about nouns (big, beautiful, expensive, happy).

**Examples:**
- A **tall** building
- An **interesting** book
- **Fresh** vegetables
- **Friendly** people

### üìï Adverbs
Adverbs tell us more about verbs, adjectives, or other adverbs (quickly, very, extremely).

**Types:**
- **Manner:** carefully, loudly, well
- **Time:** now, yesterday, soon
- **Place:** here, there, everywhere
- **Frequency:** always, often, never

### üìì Pronouns
Pronouns replace nouns to avoid repetition (I, you, he, she, it, we, they, this, that).

**Examples:**
- **John** is a teacher ‚Üí **He** is a teacher
- I love **my sister** ‚Üí I love **her**
- Where is **the book**? ‚Üí Where is **it**?

### üìî Prepositions
Prepositions show relationships between words (at, in, on, from, to, with).

**Common Uses:**
- Time: **at** 5pm, **on** Monday, **in** January
- Place: **at** home, **on** the table, **in** the box
- Direction: **to** school, **from** London

### üìí Determiners
Determiners start noun phrases (the, a, an, this, that, my, some).

**Examples:**
- **The** book (specific)
- **A** book (any book)
- **This** pen (near me)
- **My** car (possession)

### üì∞ Conjunctions
Conjunctions join parts of sentences (and, but, or, because, although).

**Examples:**
- I like tea **and** coffee
- She is tired **but** happy
- Stay home **or** go out
- I'm leaving **because** it's late

### üìå Interjections
Short exclamations (Oh! Wow! Ouch! Hey! Ah!).

## 2. Sentence Structure

### Basic Sentence Patterns

**1. Subject + Verb**
- Birds fly
- Time passes

**2. Subject + Verb + Object**
- She reads books
- They play football

**3. Subject + Verb + Complement**
- He is a teacher
- The soup tastes delicious

**4. Subject + Verb + Object + Object**
- I gave him a gift
- She told me a story

### Types of Sentences

**Declarative (Statement):**
- I love English grammar.

**Interrogative (Question):**
- Do you love English grammar?

**Imperative (Command):**
- Study English grammar!

**Exclamatory:**
- What amazing grammar this is!

## 3. Verb Tenses Overview

### Present Tenses

**Present Simple**
- **Use:** Habits, facts, routines
- **Form:** I work, She works
- **Example:** I drink coffee every morning

**Present Continuous**
- **Use:** Actions happening now
- **Form:** I am working, She is working
- **Example:** I am studying right now

**Present Perfect**
- **Use:** Past actions with present results
- **Form:** I have worked, She has worked
- **Example:** I have lived here for 5 years

**Present Perfect Continuous**
- **Use:** Actions that started in past and continue
- **Form:** I have been working
- **Example:** I have been studying for 2 hours

### Past Tenses

**Past Simple**
- **Use:** Completed actions in the past
- **Form:** I worked, She worked
- **Example:** I visited Paris last year

**Past Continuous**
- **Use:** Actions in progress in the past
- **Form:** I was working
- **Example:** I was sleeping at midnight

**Past Perfect**
- **Use:** Action before another past action
- **Form:** I had worked
- **Example:** I had finished before he arrived

**Past Perfect Continuous**
- **Use:** Continuous action before past point
- **Form:** I had been working
- **Example:** I had been waiting for an hour

### Future Tenses

**Future Simple (will)**
- **Use:** Predictions, decisions
- **Form:** I will work
- **Example:** I will call you tomorrow

**Future (going to)**
- **Use:** Plans, intentions
- **Form:** I am going to work
- **Example:** I am going to study tonight

**Future Continuous**
- **Use:** Actions in progress in future
- **Form:** I will be working
- **Example:** I will be sleeping at midnight

**Future Perfect**
- **Use:** Completed before future time
- **Form:** I will have worked
- **Example:** I will have finished by 6pm

## 4. Common Grammar Topics

### Subject-Verb Agreement
The subject and verb must agree in number.

**Rules:**
- Singular subject ‚Üí singular verb: She **walks**
- Plural subject ‚Üí plural verb: They **walk**
- Everyone, somebody, nobody ‚Üí singular: Everyone **is** here

### Articles (a, an, the)

**A/An (Indefinite):**
- Use for general, non-specific things
- **A** before consonant sounds: a car
- **An** before vowel sounds: an apple

**The (Definite):**
- Use for specific things
- The book on the table
- The sun (only one)

### Conditionals

**Zero Conditional:** General truths
- If you heat water, it boils

**First Conditional:** Real possibility
- If it rains, I will stay home

**Second Conditional:** Unreal/Hypothetical
- If I won the lottery, I would travel

**Third Conditional:** Past unreal
- If I had studied, I would have passed

### Passive Voice

**Active:** John writes the letter  
**Passive:** The letter is written by John

**When to use:**
- Focus on action, not doer
- Doer unknown or unimportant
- Formal/scientific writing

### Reported Speech

**Direct:** She said, "I am tired"  
**Reported:** She said (that) she was tired

**Changes:**
- Present ‚Üí Past
- Will ‚Üí Would
- Can ‚Üí Could
- This ‚Üí That
- Here ‚Üí There

## Common Grammar Mistakes to Avoid

‚ùå **Your vs You're**
- Your = possession: Your book
- You're = you are: You're welcome

‚ùå **Its vs It's**
- Its = possession: Its color
- It's = it is: It's raining

‚ùå **Their, There, They're**
- Their = possession: Their house
- There = place: Over there
- They're = they are: They're happy

‚ùå **Affect vs Effect**
- Affect = verb: It affects me
- Effect = noun: The effect is clear

## Grammar Rules

### Positive Sentences
**Structure:** Subject + Base Verb (+ s/es for he/she/it)

| Subject | Verb | Example |
|---------|------|---------|
| I/You/We/They | work | I work in an office. |
| He/She/It | works | She works at home. |

### Negative Sentences
**Structure:** Subject + do/does + not + base verb

- I **do not** (don't) like spicy food.
- He **does not** (doesn't) speak French.
- They **do not** (don't) play tennis.

### Questions
**Structure:** Do/Does + subject + base verb?

- **Do** you live in London?
- **Does** she work here?
- **Do** they have a car?

## Common Mistakes

‚ùå She go to school. ‚Üí ‚úÖ She **goes** to school.

‚ùå Do he speak English? ‚Üí ‚úÖ **Does** he speak English?
