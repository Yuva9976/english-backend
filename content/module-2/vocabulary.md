# Essential Phrasal Verbs for Everyday English

Phrasal verbs are combinations of verbs with prepositions or adverbs. They're very common in spoken English and often have different meanings from the individual words.

## Common Phrasal Verbs

### 1. Look after
**Meaning:** Take care of someone or something
**Examples:**
- My sister **looks after** my children when I'm at work.
- Can you **look after** my bag while I go to the bathroom?
- Nurses **look after** patients in hospitals.

### 2. Give up
**Meaning:** Stop trying; quit something
**Examples:**
- Don't **give up**! Keep practicing your English.
- He **gave up** smoking last year.
- I'll never **give up** on my dreams.

### 3. Turn down
**Meaning:** 1) Reject or refuse; 2) Lower the volume
**Examples:**
- She **turned down** the job offer because the salary was too low.
- Can you **turn down** the music? It's too loud.
- They **turned down** my request for a day off.

### 4. Get along (with)
**Meaning:** Have a good relationship with someone
**Examples:**
- I **get along** well with my colleagues.
- Do you **get along** with your neighbors?
- My brother and I don't always **get along**.

### 5. Put off
**Meaning:** Postpone; delay something
**Examples:**
- We had to **put off** the meeting until next week.
- Don't **put off** studying until the last minute!
- The game was **put off** due to bad weather.

### 6. Run out (of)
**Meaning:** Use all of something; have none left
**Examples:**
- We've **run out of** milk. Can you buy some?
- I'm **running out of** time to finish this project.
- The store has **run out of** that product.

### 7. Take off
**Meaning:** 1) Remove clothing; 2) Leave the ground (plane); 3) Become successful
**Examples:**
- Please **take off** your shoes before entering.
- The plane will **take off** in 10 minutes.
- Her business really **took off** last year.

### 8. Bring up
**Meaning:** 1) Raise a child; 2) Mention a topic
**Examples:**
- She was **brought up** by her grandparents.
- Don't **bring up** that subject at dinner!
- He **brought up** an interesting point in the meeting.

### 9. Come across
**Meaning:** Find something by chance; give an impression
**Examples:**
- I **came across** an old photo album in the attic.
- She **comes across** as very confident.
- Have you **come across** this problem before?

### 10. Work out
**Meaning:** 1) Exercise; 2) Find a solution; 3) Calculate
**Examples:**
- I **work out** at the gym three times a week.
- Things will **work out** in the end, don't worry.
- Can you **work out** the total cost?
