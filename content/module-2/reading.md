# Reading Comprehension: The Weekend Adventure

Reading helps you learn new vocabulary, grammar, and understand English culture. Read the story below and answer the questions.

## üìñ Story: Emma's Saturday

It was a beautiful Saturday morning in London. Emma woke up early and looked out of her bedroom window. The sun was shining and the sky was blue. "Perfect weather for an adventure!" she thought.

Emma loved exploring new places in the city. Today, she decided to visit the famous Borough Market. She had heard a lot about it from her friends, but she had never been there before.

After a quick breakfast of toast and coffee, Emma got dressed and left her apartment. She took the underground train to London Bridge station. The journey took about 20 minutes. When she arrived, she followed the crowd of people walking towards the market.

Borough Market was incredible! There were hundreds of food stalls selling everything you can imagine: fresh fruits and vegetables, cheese from France, Italian pasta, Spanish olives, and beautiful flowers. The colors and smells were amazing. Emma felt like she was traveling around the world without leaving London!

She stopped at a small stall selling homemade chocolate. "Would you like to try a sample?" asked the friendly vendor. Emma tried a piece of dark chocolate with sea salt. It was delicious! She bought a box to take home.

Next, Emma walked to a bakery stall. The bread looked so fresh and smelled wonderful. She bought a sourdough loaf for her lunch. As she continued walking, she met an old friend from university, Sarah, who was also visiting the market.

"Emma! What a surprise!" Sarah said with a big smile. They hugged and decided to have lunch together at a small caf√© nearby. They ordered fish and chips and talked for hours about their jobs, families, and travel plans.

After lunch, Emma and Sarah walked along the River Thames. They took photos of the beautiful view with Tower Bridge in the background. Emma felt happy and relaxed. It had been a perfect Saturday.

At 5 o'clock, Emma said goodbye to Sarah and took the train home. She was tired but very happy. As she walked into her apartment, she thought, "London is such a wonderful city. There's always something new to discover!"

## üìö Reading Tips

1. **Read the questions first** - Know what information to look for
2. **Don't worry about every word** - Focus on understanding the main ideas
3. **Use context clues** - Guess the meaning of new words from the sentences around them
4. **Read multiple times** - First for general understanding, then for details
5. **Time yourself** - Practice reading faster to improve your speed
